I want to create a virtual board game using the gameboard.webp file as the game board for the game.

The game will be playable for 1 to 6 players who will be able to set their name in the game and choose a starting Pokémon from the first generation of Pokémon. This can be Charmander, Squirtle, or Bulbasaur. 

During a players turn, they roll a single six sided die and move that amount of spaces on the game board.

The spaces are the white, grey and yellow squares that are around the board. There are 72 spaces in total. 

Try and parse the squares so they can be their own spot to land on. If you can read the text for each space we will want to show that text to the player when they land on that space.

Players start in Pallet Town in the bottom left of the board and move up, going in a spiral shape around the board as the game progresses.

Once a player reaches a yellow space, they are required to stop regardless of if they still have movement left or not.

If a player lands at the start of a grey section of squares, they will have to follow the rules, given by the text of the square, for as long as they are in that same stretch of grey squares. Once they move past the set of grey squares, that rule no longer applies to them.

If two players land on the same white square they will have to battle. A battle requires each player to roll a six sided die and the higher roll wins. An exception to this is that your starting Pokémon gives you an advantage or disadvantage in battle. If Squirtle battles Charmander, Squirtle will get 2 rolls and Charmander will only get 1 roll. If Charmander battles Bulbasaur, Charmander will get 2 rolls and Bulbasaur will only get 1 roll. If Bulbasaur battles Squirtle, Bulbasaur will get 2 rolls and Squirtle will only get 1 roll. If Pikachu battles Squirtle, Pikachu will get 2 rolls and Squirtle will only get 1 roll. The loser takes 2 drinks. If it's a tie, both players take a drink.
